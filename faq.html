Frequently Asked Questions

What is deception technology? ▼

Deception technology uses fake assets (honeypots, canary tokens, decoys) to detect attackers. Any interaction with a deception asset is malicious by definition, resulting in zero false positives.

How is Kavach v1 different from canarytokens.org? ▼

Canarytokens.org is a free, community tool. Kavach v1 adds:

  • Real-time attacker fingerprinting (OS, browser, geolocation)
  • Integrations (Slack, Datadog, webhooks)
  • Correlation analysis (linking attacks across tokens)
  • Team management & audit logs
  • 90-day log retention (vs 7 days free)

How does the ML classifier in Kavach v2 work? ▼

Kavach v2 classifies traffic across 5 dimensions:

  • IP Reputation: Is the source IP known malicious?
  • Request Patterns: Is the request rate/frequency abnormal?
  • Payload Analysis: Does the request contain attack signatures?
  • Header Fingerprinting: Browser, OS, TLS, network metadata
  • Behavioral Anomalies: Does this violate your baseline?

Each dimension scores 0-20 points. Total: 0-100. Routing: <31 → real app, 31-70 → honeypot, 71-100 → block.

Do you store my application data? ▼

No. We only store:

  • Token metadata (type, name, creation date)
  • Attack events (timestamp, IP, attacker fingerprint)
  • Your account info (email, team members)

    • We never store or see your real application code, data, or users. You own 100% of your data and can export anytime.
